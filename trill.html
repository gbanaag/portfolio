Trill Group Chats

"How do you connect users together in
small groups on a social network?"

Role

Product Intern

Timeframe

July 2020 - August 2020

Team

1 Product Design Intern, 1 Product Manager, 1 UX Research Intern

Deliverable

High-fidelity wireframes, Working prototype

Trill is a peer support network.

Trill Project is a social networking platform available on mobile and web that creates a virtual support community with a focus on inclusivity, privacy, and anonymity.

Users can anonymously express themselves and connect with other users on the application.

Users wanted to build their own small communities.

While the app supported directly messaging other users, finding users through posts, and joining existing communities within the network, users were unable to create communities and groups of their own.

Through monthly surveys, Trill's beta testers expressed wanting a group chat feature so they were able to connect with more people at once through mutual friends and interests.

I was tasked with designing the ability to create and use group chats with other users.

Our team believed that implementing a group chat feature within the app would provide increased social support, greater app engagement, and an overall more personalized user experience.

Because a lot of social media apps already have group chats, I took note of what a lot of what features their group chats had in common and implemented frequently used ones into the Trill app.
